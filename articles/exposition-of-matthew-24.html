Throne of his Father David

Author

Brian Onang’o

Is it without reason that God chooses to be identified with certain men more than others? It is true that “God is no respecter of persons,” so that “in every nation he that feareth him, and worketh righteousness, is accepted with him”. Acts 10:34. To every faithful child of His He says, “I [am] the LORD thy God”. Exodus 20:2. But it is also true that He identifies Himself more closely with some men than with others. By association He is more the God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob than of anyone else (). As well he was the God of Israel than of any other nation (). It was by a covenant that He became the God of Israel and when they broke the covenant He divorced them. Therefore this assocition with God is only gained and maintained by faithfulness.

This is true also for the relationship between Christ and God. In the first chapter of Hebrews, because Jesus Christ “loved righteousness, and hated iniquity” (verse 9), God says to Him that He is God, “even thy(Christ’s) God”. The full verse reads, “Thou hast loved righteousness, and hated iniquity; therefore God, [even] thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows.” Christ came to this world a fallible human being of the seed of fallible Abraham. (Heb. 2:16) In nature He could be no better than Abraham. And had he sinned in any particular, God would have ceased being His God.

From which tribe was Christ to Come?? Gen 49:10

Judah was not taken captive.

The Messiah was to be of the royal line, for in the prophecy uttered by Jacob the Lord said, “The scepter shall not depart from Judah, nor a lawgiver from between his feet, until Shiloh come; and unto Him shall the gathering of the people be.” Genesis 49:10. {AA 223.1}

“The sceptre shall not depart from Judah, nor a lawgiver from between his feet, until Shiloh come; and unto him shall the gathering of the people be.” This prophecy was uttered by the dying Jacob, and addressed to his sons. But it bore with weight upon future generations. . . . The scepter is a rod carried in the hands of chiefs and rulers as a badge of authority. . . .“The sceptre shall not depart from Judah.” The ensign of his tribeship shall remain. Judah shall not cease to be a distinct tribe until Shiloh come. . . .And the tribe of Judah did maintain its supremacy through all its adversities till Christ’s first advent. It remained a distinct tribe till Shiloh came. {CTr 93.5}
